# Week 2 Reflection: Learning in Public and Letting the Process Show

This week felt like a turning point.

Not because everything suddenly became easy, but because things started to make more sense. I spent less time overthinking and more time doing, testing, and documenting.

Here are the key lessons and moments that stood out.

![Week 2 Reflection](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Uxer-Janine/outreachy-docling-task/master/06-images/08-week2-reflection.png)

---

## 1. Learning to Contribute Publicly

One of my biggest shifts this week was understanding the importance of working in public.

- I started sharing my progress more openly  
- I became less concerned about things being “perfect” before posting  
- I realized that showing the process is just as important as showing results  

This was a big mindset change.

Open source is not about polished final work. It is about:
- thinking out loud  
- documenting decisions  
- allowing others to follow and contribute  

---

## 2. Small Details Matter More Than You Think

A small but important moment this week came from something simple: commit messages.

- I had been writing them in past tense  
- A friend pointed out that they should be written in present tense  

It seemed minor at first, but it made me more aware of how:

- conventions exist for a reason  
- consistency improves collaboration  
- even small details reflect how well you understand a workflow  

---

## 3. Debugging is Part of the Work

This week reinforced something I am starting to accept:

- things will break  
- tools will behave unexpectedly  
- and that is normal  

Instead of rushing to fix things blindly, I focused more on:
- reading errors carefully  
- understanding what was actually happening  
- making deliberate fixes  

This made the process feel more controlled and less frustrating.

---

## 4. From Following Instructions to Understanding Systems

Earlier on, I was mostly focused on completing tasks.

This week, that started to change.

I found myself:
- thinking about how different parts of the pipeline connect  
- questioning why certain steps are necessary  
- comparing tools instead of just using them  

This shift from “doing” to “understanding” has been important.

---

## 5. Confidence Through Practice

This is my fourth time applying to Outreachy, but this is the first time I have actually enjoyed the contribution process.

The difference is clear:

- I have built skills over time  
- I am more comfortable experimenting  
- I trust myself to figure things out  

There is still a lot to learn, but it no longer feels intimidating.

---

## Final Thoughts

This week was less about building things and more about learning how to work effectively.

- how to communicate  
- how to document  
- how to share progress  
- and how to stay consistent  

More than anything, I am starting to see open source as a space I can grow in, not one I need to prove myself worthy of.

---
