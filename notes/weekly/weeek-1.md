## Overview  

This week was mostly about figuring things out — what the project is, what’s expected, and whether I was even ready for it.

I started by going through all the available Outreachy projects and trying to find something that matched my skills. The Ramalama RAG project stood out almost immediately because it combined AI with real-world applications, which is something I’ve been wanting to explore more deeply.

At the same time, I felt a lot of uncertainty. Many of the tools and concepts were completely new to me, and I wasn’t sure where to even begin. But as I spent more time reading and interacting with the community, things slowly started to make more sense.

## What I Learned  

- RAG is basically about retrieving the right information first, then generating answers from it  
- OCR allows computers to read text from images or scanned documents  
- Docling helps turn messy documents into something structured and usable for AI  
- You don’t need to understand everything at once — things start to click as you go  

---

## Challenges  

The biggest challenge this week was honestly **feeling overwhelmed**.

There were a lot of new terms, tools, and systems all at once (Ramalama, RAG, Fedora packaging, Docling) and it felt like I was expected to understand everything immediately.

I also dealt with a fair amount of **imposter syndrome**. At some point, I genuinely questioned whether I was qualified to be working on this project.

On top of that, even simple setup tasks took longer than expected. Things like updating my profile or fixing visibility settings ended up taking hours because I missed small details in the instructions.

---

## How I Handled It  

Instead of trying to understand everything at once, I started breaking things down into smaller pieces.

For example, instead of thinking about the full RAG pipeline, I focused on:
- first understanding OCR  
- then how text gets structured  
- then how tools like Docling fit in  

I also reminded myself that this is a **learning experience**, not an exam.

That shift in mindset made a big difference.

---

## Reflections  

I almost gave up on this project at one point.

Everything felt unfamiliar, and I wasn’t sure I could keep up. But taking a step back and coming back to it later helped me see things more clearly.

One thing that really stood out this week is how supportive the Fedora community is. Knowing that there are people you can ask for help makes it much easier to keep going.

Also, I’m starting to understand that progress here isn’t about knowing everything, it’s about **showing up, trying, and learning in public**.
